Dashboard guide
How to read the cohort retention chart
There's your Threadly dashboard — one scrollable page, all five sections the brief called for.

Each row is everyone who made their first purchase in that month. M0 is always 100% (by definition — that's when they first bought). M1 through M11 show what % of that cohort came back and ordered in each subsequent month. Darker green = better retention.
1
The Sep–Nov 2025 cohorts retain meaningfully better than earlier ones — something changed in acquisition quality, worth investigating.
2
By month 6, cohorts stabilize around 20–25% — that's Threadly's "true" loyal customer rate and what LTV should be modeled on.
Case study
🔥
The problem
A D2C fashion brand doing ₹87L/month couldn't tell which SKUs were actually making money.

Revenue reports showed the bestselling products — but margin, cohort retention, and geographic concentration were invisible. The team was optimising for top-line while contribution margin quietly eroded.

Without a unified view, every ad-spend decision was a guess.
⚙️
What I built
  • SKU-level margin analysis that surfaces the 10 highest-revenue and 10 highest-margin products side by side
  • Cohort retention heatmap tracking every first-time buyer cohort through 12 months of repeat purchase behaviour
  • State-level revenue breakdown with fastest-growing cities and highest-AOV markets called out
  • 30-day Prophet forecast with 80% confidence band — ₹94–108L projected for the next 30 days
📈
The outcome
  • Discovered the bestseller tee had only 14% margin while Linen Kurta Set had 58% — ranked #7 in revenue
  • Shifted ₹4L/month ad spend toward high-margin SKUs
  • Contribution margin improved by 28%
  • Identified Sep–Nov 2025 as a cohort quality inflection point, enabling the team to double down on acquisition channels that were working
