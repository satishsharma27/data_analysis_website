Here's the logic behind how I split the ₹47,200:

The rule I used: put money where marginal ROAS is highest, not where current ROAS is highest.

Those two things sound the same but they're not. A campaign sitting at 5.2x ROAS today might only hold 3x if you triple its budget — diminishing returns. So the question isn't "which campaign has the best ROAS?" It's "which campaign can absorb more money without its ROAS collapsing?"

That's why the split isn't evenly distributed across the top 3:

Retargeting got the biggest chunk (₹15,000) because warm audiences are the cheapest conversions and it's currently under-frequency — room to grow.

Brand search got ₹12,200 because branded keyword traffic is capped by budget, not demand. Every rupee here captures someone already searching for Kaya by name. You literally can't find cheaper customers.

Proven influencers got ₹12,000 instead of throwing more money at random creators.

₹8,000 goes to a test — always reserve 15–20% of reallocations for testing new creatives or angles. Without tests, the account stagnates.
